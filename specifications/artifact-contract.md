# 1. Purpose

The Artifact Contract defines the governance, structure, lifecycle, validation, versioning, and traceability requirements for every artifact generated by the EditorialAfrica AI Platform.

Artifacts are the permanent outputs of pipeline execution and represent the platform's business deliverables.

This contract ensures every generated artifact is consistent, reproducible, discoverable, and traceable.

---

# 2. Scope

This contract applies to every generated artifact including:

- Editorial Summary
- Story Ranking
- Comparative Analysis
- Enterprise Intelligence
- Facebook Post
- LinkedIn Post
- Instagram Caption
- TikTok Script
- X Post
- HeyGen Script
- Sources Manifest
- Execution Report

Future artifacts SHALL conform to this contract.

---

# 3. Definition

An Artifact is a versioned, immutable output generated by an AI Agent during pipeline execution.

Artifacts SHALL:

- represent business outputs
- be reproducible
- be versioned
- be traceable
- be validated

Artifacts SHALL NOT:

- contain executable logic
- modify contexts
- alter pipeline behaviour

---

# 4. Design Principles

## 4.1 Immutable

Artifacts SHALL NOT be modified after generation.

Corrections SHALL produce a new artifact.

---

## 4.2 Traceable

Every artifact SHALL identify:

- producing agent
- pipeline execution
- source context
- generation timestamp
- version

---

## 4.3 Deterministic

Identical inputs SHOULD produce identical artifacts.

---

## 4.4 Self-Describing

Every artifact SHALL contain sufficient metadata to describe its origin and purpose.

---

## 4.5 Reproducible

Artifacts SHALL be reproducible from the same:

- source editorials
- configuration
- contexts
- pipeline version

# 8. Artifact Naming

Artifact names SHALL be unique.

Naming SHALL follow repository conventions defined in:

```
config/filenames.json
```

Hardcoded filenames SHALL NOT be used.

---

# 9. Artifact Generation

Artifacts SHALL only be generated by registered AI agents.

Every generated artifact SHALL be declared within the producing agent.

Undeclared artifacts SHALL fail validation.

---

# 10. Artifact Registration

Every artifact SHALL be registered.

Registration SHALL include:

- name
- producing agent
- category
- version
- timestamp
- validation status

Registration SHALL be governed by:

```
config/artifacts.json
```

---

# 11. Validation

Artifacts SHALL be validated before registration.

Validation SHALL verify:

- metadata
- required sections
- formatting
- completeness
- naming
- traceability

Validation failures SHALL be reported to the Pipeline Engine.

Artifacts are the durable business outputs of the EditorialAfrica AI Platform. They encapsulate the results of editorial analysis, publishing preparation, operational reporting, and other platform capabilities.
