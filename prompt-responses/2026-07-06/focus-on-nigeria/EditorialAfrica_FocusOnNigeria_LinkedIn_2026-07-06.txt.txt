The honeymoon phase is over, and the Nigerian press is demanding accountability. 🇳🇬

In today's edition of #FocusOnNigeria, we dissect the heaviest themes from the nation's leading editorial boards. The central narrative across the board? A profound crisis of implementation and transparency as the administration navigates its first term.

Key takeaways from our Monday media analysis:
🔹 The Gbajagate Scandal: Punch Newspapers raises serious red flags over leaked memos regarding a N1.3 billion budget allocation for the supposedly non-existent Presidential Foreign Intervention Promotion Council (PFIPC). At a time of economic austerity, executive transparency is non-negotiable. 
🔹 Economic Squeeze: BusinessDay warns that the CBN's hawkish interest rates, while stabilizing the Naira, are actively suffocating local SMEs and manufacturing. Are we risking an industrial recession to fight inflation?
🔹 Local Government Autonomy: The Nation calls out state governors for continuing to bypass the Supreme Court's ruling on JAAC, starving the grassroots of vital infrastructure.

The media is sending a clear message: structural reforms must move from paper to reality.
