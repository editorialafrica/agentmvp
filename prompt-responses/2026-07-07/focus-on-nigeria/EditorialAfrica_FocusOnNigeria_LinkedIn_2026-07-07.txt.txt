The disconnect between federal policies and state-level execution in Nigeria is widening. 🇳🇬

In today's Tuesday edition of #FocusOnNigeria, we dissect the most critical themes from the nation's leading editorial boards. The central narrative is clear: an administration struggling with accountability and powerful state executives bypassing the rule of law.

Key takeaways from our media analysis:
🔹 The Gbajagate Scandal: Punch Newspapers demands answers regarding a N1.3 billion budget allocation for the supposedly non-existent "PFIPC". True economic reform requires absolute executive transparency.
🔹 Sabotaging Autonomy: The Nation exposes how state assemblies are being used by Governors to pass parallel laws, effectively funneling newly liberated Local Government funds right back into state joint accounts.
🔹 The Security & Food Nexus: Daily Trust urgently reignites the State Police debate, arguing that centralized military deployments are failing to protect the North-West's agrarian belt, risking a nationwide food crisis.
🔹 The Wage Mirage: BusinessDay highlights the paradox of state governors receiving record-high FAAC allocations yet declaring bankruptcy when asked to pay the new minimum wage.
