Punch is demanding answers to a very simple question: How do you appropriate over a billion Naira to a ghost? The editorial board argues that the administration’s refusal to address the "Gbajagate" scandal is not just poor public relations; it is actively fueling public suspicion that high-ranking officials are being shielded from accountability.

The editorial details a clandestine effort across the country where state houses of assembly are being pressured to pass parallel, localized laws. The message is clear: true democracy will never reach the rural masses until the third tier of government is financially liberated from imperial state governors.

Federal troops, no matter how heavily armed, often lack the localized intelligence, the geographical knowledge, and the linguistic fluency required to root out entrenched bandit syndicates.

Yes, the board acknowledges the valid fears that state governors might weaponize local police against political opponents ahead of the 2027 elections. However, the paper argues that the immediate, existential threat of a nationwide famine—driven by farmers who can no longer plant their crops—far outweighs these political risks. We are facing a food crisis, and the current security architecture is simply unfit for purpose.
