Is institutional accountability failing in Nigeria? The press is demanding answers. 🇳🇬

In today’s Thursday edition of #FocusOnNigeria, we examine the heavy sense of institutional fatigue reported across the nation's leading editorial boards as the Tinubu administration navigates the second half of its first term.

Key takeaways from our media analysis:
🔹 The EFCC's Silence: Punch Newspapers issues a scathing indictment of the EFCC for failing to launch a suo motu investigation into the N1.3 billion "Gbajagate" ghost agency scandal. Selective anti-corruption measures damage investor and public confidence.
🔹 The Wage Ultimatum: With a 72-hour NLC strike looming, The Nation urges the Federal Government to unconditionally withhold FAAC allocations to state governors who refuse to pay the minimum wage while approving luxury expenditures. 
🔹 Economic Brinkmanship: The Guardian highlights the macroeconomic damage of the 6th national grid collapse of 2026, while BusinessDay warns the CBN that cracking down on BDCs won't solve the structural lack of dollar liquidity.
🔹 Political Reckoning: ThisDay notes that tomorrow's Federal High Court verdict on the PDP's leadership crisis could dictate the trajectory of Nigeria's democracy ahead of 2027.

The media consensus is clear: Nigeria cannot afford reactive governance. Structural reform is urgently needed.
